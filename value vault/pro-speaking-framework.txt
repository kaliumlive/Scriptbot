Title: 5 Professional Speaking Mistakes (Building Connection on Camera)

Goal: Build deep connection with the audience through authentic and confident speaking.

Mistake #1: Breaking the Coffee Shop Rule
- If it feels weird to say it to a friend in a cafe, it will feel weird on camera.
- Shift from "presentation" to "conversation".
- Fix: Do a "coffee shop check" — would I talk to a friend like this?

Mistake #2: Perfecting versus Connecting
- Focus on connecting with the person behind the lens rather than trying to be perfect.
- Perceived judgment causes "deer in the headlights" stiffness.
- Fix: Inner mindset shift — "I am here to connect, not to be judged."

Mistake #3: Emotional Spaghetti
- Confusing the audience by not knowing how you want them to feel.
- Vulnerability needs a purpose (inspiration vs. processed lesson).
- Fix: Decide on the target emotion (inspired, motivated, etc.) BEFORE starting.

Mistake #4: All Points and No Paint
- Making listicle points without anchoring them in relatable analogies/metaphors.
- Fix: Use "Paint" (analogies, stories, examples) for every "Point".
- Example: Machine learning vs. teaching a child what a dog is.

Mistake #5: Going In Cold
- High-energy communication requires physical and mental activation.
- Fix: Warm up body (shaking out, vocal exercises) and mind (visualization of impact).
- State Control: Elevate consciousness and energy before recording.

Key Deliverable: One-page cheat sheet for camera confidence.
