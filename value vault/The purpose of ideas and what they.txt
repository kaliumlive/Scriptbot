## 2️⃣Two main methods for idea generation

We have understood the importance of a good idea when it comes to social media success; now let’s explore the **2 main methods for idea generation:**

<aside>
1️⃣ Passive (Eureka Moment)

</aside>

These are the so-called **“Aha” moments** in which suddenly you get a groundbreaking idea

**It often happens in the moments you least expect** - when you drive, shower, or are trying to fall asleep…

There are multiple reasons why this happens, but in short, it looks like this:

![Untitled-2.png](https://prod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/1ff374e6-af95-4f0b-8a40-b4d61932681b/7741a322-008c-436c-a13c-4a9cbf5cd78e/e8744862-e0a1-4229-b8c2-fab73720a2f5.png)

To get these “Aha” moments more predictably, one must go through a **3 stage process:**

(1) Prime your brain > (2) Seek alone time with active rest > (3) Be patient

**1️⃣Priming your brain** - the quality of your output is determined by the quality of your input. By consuming great content, you are more likely to get ideas on how to produce great content. And it doesn’t only have to be social media posts; it can be anything. Movies, books, music or content from entirely unrelated niches.

2️⃣ **Seek active rest** - going for a walk, driving a car, playing with your dogs, taking a shower - tasks that occupy your body but don’t require you to think about them actively and are often without distractions

3️⃣ **Be patient** - the nature of Eureka moments is that you don’t know when they will happen - the more you chase them, the more elusive they will be, so just relax, be patient and let these ideas naturally happen

**PRO tip:** 

Once an idea arises passively - **write it down or say it to someone as fast as possible, or you risk losing it forever.** I have lost countless ideas I got when falling asleep, naively thinking I’d remember them when I woke up

## 3️⃣Idea Generation Through Inspiration

The best way to come up with **great ideas is by consuming great ideas first**

If you learn how to do this effectively, this is essentially a cheat code for learning content creation

Because you can learn not only from your posts but from the posts of other people

I call it Competitive Benchmarking

I dive [**super deep into it in my book,**](https://bit.ly/Harry10KGuide) but here’s the summary: 

1️⃣**•Identify profiles that have your audience**

2️⃣**•Look at their last 30 posts and check their average likes** (you can use ViralFindr)

3️⃣**•Look for posts that have 2x more likes AND 2x less likes**

4️⃣**•Go through each part of the post (idea, caption, design, comments, information) and try to understand why the post failed or went viral**

Here’s an example of how this **competitive benchmarking** looks like: 

![Untitled](https://prod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/1ff374e6-af95-4f0b-8a40-b4d61932681b/c5bc2ac8-9b52-412e-9de7-8496bb5d84b7/Untitled.png)

Do this for **10 profiles each quarter with 3 viral posts and 3 bad posts** 

It’s important to note that after you’ve identified what works for competitors, **you don’t want to copy/paste it**

Even if you did, it’s not going to produce the same results for you

Instead, you want to make sure you **understand why the hooks worked/failed** and put your own spin on them

There is a great book on the topic which I heavily recommend: **“Steal like an Artist”**

## 4️⃣Two AI Prompts for Idea Generation:

If you are down badly and you need good hooks fast, **AI can help you out**

Again, important to note here is that you **don’t want to copy/paste everything AI gives you** 

I like looking at working with AI through the **lens of cooking**

Unless you are a prodigy or have done a recipe 1000 times, you never just throw ingredients together and immediately serve

If you want to produce a **delicious dish,** you must taste it and refine it further

**It’s the same with AI - you should curate the results** 

Use these prompts, see what AI spills out, and **then add a pinch of your unique sauce to make something truly powerful:**

## 5️⃣Checklist for the perfect content idea

- [ ]  Does it reflect a **pain point/benefit your audience wants?**
- [ ]  **Does it serve your goal** as a creator?
- [ ]  **No** technical words/**jargon**
- [ ]  Is it **short and simple**, so even a cold audience will get it?
- [ ]  Does it make you want to **consume the rest of the post?**
- [ ]  Does it **accurately describe the value** of the rest of the post?
- [ ]  If it uses a question, **is the answer not obvious?**
- [ ]  Does the language used in the **hook resonate with your tone of voice?**
