# The 6 Core Principles of Persuasion

**Authority** — People will follow and listen to you if you have authority, credibility, and expertise.

**Social Proof** — People will follow and listen to you if they see that others are too.

**Liking** — People will follow and listen to you if they like you.

**Commitment/Consistency** — People act in alignment with their identity and self-image.

**Reciprocity** — People feel obligated to return favors.

**Scarcity/Urgency** — The less of something there is, the more we want it.

# The Hook 🪝

The hook is the entire concept for the video, so obviously, that’s where you should start.

Instead of staring at a blank page and feeling overwhelmed by having to write an entire script,

…all you have to do really is **just write one sentence**, and you’re halfway done, because now you at least know what the entire concept for the video is.

Once you have the concept for the video, it’s basically already written, literally just riff on the concept, and throw in hella psychology tricks.

---

## Tips & Tricks for Hooks:

- Make it **psychologically impossible** for them not to watch the rest of the video.
- Make them crave the **resolution/payoff** to the **premise** you just introduced.
- There are many different ways to do this:
    - Burning question
    - Dream outcome
    - Pain points
    - etc.
- For videos that are meant to go viral, the hook needs **mass appeal**.

## Weak Hook Examples:

***“If you’re not using this one tool to source profitable products for your online arbitrage business, then you’re miles behind the competition”***

WAY too specific, no mass appeal

***“This is how you’re gonna use your phone to make an extra $2k per month”***

bland, generic

***“You’re not gonna solve your problems by avoiding the actual work”***

I think this was an attempt to be controversial, but it’s literally just a normal sentence.

## Weak vs. Strong Hook

***“This side hustle made me $10,000 in the last 6 months”*** ❌

vs.

***“Testing side hustles part 1”***

***“Everyone says I can make $1000s a day dropshipping, so let’s see if that’s true!”*** ✅

# Build-Up 🏗️

Alright so we hooked them in, they’re curious about the resolution to the story, but we need them to be EVEN more committed to the video.

We need to make them **CRAVE** the resolution/payoff.

So here’s where you just **hype up** the **value**/**resolution/payoff** even more and **build more tension**, **build more suspense.**

**Mind Read** - Figure out what the viewer will assume and then immediately negate it.

***“And NO, I didn’t …”***

***“And before you say anything, NO it’s not ______ or ______”***

**Risk Reversal** - Make it seem like if they watch your video or follow your video’s steps, they have nothing to lose and everything to gain.

***“And the crazy thing is, this will only take a minute to set up, and will cost you less than a subway sandwich”***

***“This method is so untapped that I almost don’t even wanna give it away. But, here we go…”***

***“And it gets even better, you can start with no money and no experience. Let me put you on game”***

***“Even if you suck at ______, this will work for you, I promise”***

***“I’m going to put a lot of power in your hands, so use this at your own discretion”***

***“Watch me do it in under a minute”***

**Authority Endorsement** - Instead of backing up your hook with logic or facts, you’re backing it with someone else’s stamp of approval or end result.

***“This is the same way [Company or Person] [Desired Outcome]”***

***“This is the same secret sauce that people like Devin Jatho, Iman Gadzhi, Ali Abdaal, Eddie Cumberbatch are all using right now to crush the content game”***

**Controversial** - Either validate an opinion or challenge it. Try to get an emotional response.

***“And I know what I’m about to say might make everyone upset”***

***“Don’t get me wrong, running ads is good, but building a viral personal brand is just so much better”***

**Personal Authority** - Use your own social proof to hype up the video’s upcoming value.

***“And this is the same thing I’ve done to reach [Desired Outcome]”***

***“And this is how you can learn from my mistake so it doesn’t happen to you”***

***Main Objective:*** Build hype, build suspense.

# Value 💰

**SPECIFIC** and **ACTIONABLE**

Technical/tactical information that the viewer can immediately benefit from.

Definition of “sauce.”

This is stuff that **ONLY YOU** know, but **EVERYONE** could seriously benefit from.

- Tips
- Tricks
- Steps
- Websites
- Tools
- Software
- Advice (better be good)
- Insights
- **RESOURCES**

Make sure you **CUT OUT ALL THE FLUFF.**

***Would the viewer pay $5 for the information in this video?***

^ VERY important question that you should ALWAYS be asking when it comes to the value of your video.

---

# Main Point / Payoff 💵

This isn’t always one specific line per se, but rather, the ***moment*** when the viewer finally gets their resolution to the hook.

***NEVER*** give this away right away!!

You wanna hold off on this as long as you can, build suspense, and build a burning desire for the viewer to finally get that payoff!

---

# CTA 🏃

You just gave the viewer some crazy value, now you’re asking for a small favor in return.

BONUS POINTS if your CTA *doesn’t* ask, and instead just keeps on giving.

Law of reciprocity, they’re more likely to follow you if you give them an insanely valuable resource than if you beg like a peasant for them to follow you.

- Comment ____ for ____
- Share this with a friend who _____
- Save this video so you don’t forget next time you ____
- Follow for more value (weak)
- Hit the link in my bio (honestly, not that bad)
- Go out and USE the information I just gave you
