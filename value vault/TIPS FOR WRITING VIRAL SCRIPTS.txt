TIPS FOR WRITING VIRAL SCRIPTS

TALK TO YOUR VIEWER, BUT INCLUDE YOURSELF TOO
I encourage you to speak directly to and about your viewer to grab attention, but don’t be afraid to share your own experiences too. Relatability is key for both retention and shareability. When viewers see themselves in your story, they connect with you, trust you more, and are far more likely to share your content.

SIMPLIFY, THEN SIMPLIFY AGAIN
Once you've written a script, re-read it and cut out anything that doesn’t need to be there. Do this as many times as possible so your message gets across in the most specific and simple way (remember, 10% brain capacity).

USE TALKING POINTS FOR AUTHENTICITY
To keep it feeling more natural, you don’t need a word-forword script. Just write talking points so your own way of speaking comes through when you’re on camera.

DON’T GIVE AWAY THE PAYOFF TOO EARLY
If you reveal the solution or “big secret” at the start, viewers have no reason to stick around. Build tension first with a hook, a relatable story, or the problem your viewer is facing. ThenThen deliver the payoff at the right moment, so the audience feels rewarded for watching until the end.


VIRAL STORYTELLING SCRIPT TEMPLATE
HOOK 1:
(A bold statement, surprising fact, or pattern-interrupt that grabs attention in the
first 1 - 3 seconds)
HOOK 2:
(A follow-up hook that deepens curiosity or reinforces the problem)
RELATABLE STORY THAT ADDRESS A PROBLEM:
(A short, simple story or scenario that highlights the viewer’s struggle in a way
they can personally relate to)
SOLUTION / PAYOFF:
(Present the fix, solution, or insight clearly—make it practical, useful, and directly
relevant to the audience.)
CALL TO ACTION:
End with a strong CTA that tells the viewer exactly what to do next (e.g., comment,save, follow, download).
Bonus Tip: jot down the type of content you’re going to film or insert with your text. Having this direction makes filming quicker and helps your content turn out stronger.
